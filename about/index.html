About Me



Hello! I'm Raymond Matson, a recent PhD graduate in Mathematics from the University of California, Riverside, where I researched quantum representation theory and low-dimensional topology under the supervision of professor Peter Samuelson. Specifically, I studied stated skein algebras and how they interact and connect with double affine Hecke algebras. You can find a copy of my dissertation here. Since 2023, I have also had the pleasure of collaborating with HP Labs on cutting-edge computer networking research.


Prior to joining UC Riverside, I received my Bachelors of Science in Mathematics at UC Davis in 2017. My undergraduate research primarily focused on pursuit-evasion on graphs under the guidance of professor Erik Slivken.


In between degrees I worked for Hewlett-Packard Enterprise under the R&D division of Aruba Networks. During this time, I had hands-on experience with systems engineering, regularly using resources such as Telnet/Putty, VMWare, ESXi, MySQL, and Ixia while working in RHEL, CentOS, Ubuntu, Windows, Cisco IOS, H3C, ArubaOS, and ProCurve environments. I also gained lots of programming experience in C, C++, Tcl, Perl, Java, Python, and even some ASM. Fun fact - my unbelievably kind boss from HPE, Tim Twietmeyer, is a famous ultramarathon runner.





Outside of math, I typically fill up my spare time by playing guitar or composing songs. For any fellow guitar enthusiasts, I own two Ibanez guitars (one is an s series and the other a black knight), an LTD deluxe ec-1000, and an acoustic Gretsch 3700, which I undoubtedly seem to favor.


Once upon a time in high school a couple friends and I started a metal band, in which I played guitar. In order to maintain a minimal amount of embarrassment links will not be provided (although I'm sure if you crawl around the internet long enough you can figure it out). I still enjoy jamming with friends occasionally, although, these days I usually just write and play in my room. If anyone ever wants to talk music (bands, gear, writing, sound engineering, etc.) I'm always available.
